Storage Medium Lifespan Reality Check

Now let's see how your ideas compare to real storage technologies. Different storage methods have dramatically different lifespans!

Short-Term Storage (Years)

  • USB drives: 10-20 years (if you're lucky!)
  • Hard drives: 3-5 years with heavy use
  • SSDs: 5-10 years depending on usage
  • Cloud storage: As long as the company exists

Medium-Term Storage (Decades)

  • CDs/DVDs: 20-100 years (if stored perfectly)
  • Magnetic tape: 30-50 years
  • Printed photos: 50-200 years

Long-Term Storage (Centuries+)

  • Paper: 500-1000 years (acid-free paper)
  • Stone carvings: 1000s of years
  • DNA storage: 1000+ years (experimental)
  • Glass storage: 1 million+ years!

Big Data: The Power of Good and Evil

The Same Data, Different Intentions

Big data is neither inherently good nor evil - it's how humans choose to use it that determines its impact on society.

Using Data for Good

Traffic Management

Ever seen these tubes on the road? As an example, these black tubes collect massive amounts of traffic data to optimise traffic lights, plan safer roads, and reduce pollution through better traffic flow.

Healthcare Breakthroughs

Hospitals use big data to predict sepsis infections hours before symptoms appear, detect cancer patterns in medical images, and personalise treatments based on genetic data.

Disaster Response

Emergency services analyse mobile phone data during natural disasters to locate trapped survivors and coordinate rescue efforts more efficiently.

Climate Research

Scientists process massive datasets from satellites, weather stations, and sensors to understand climate change and develop environmental solutions.

Data Used for Harm

Surveillance Overreach

Facial recognition systems have led to wrongful arrests of innocent people, particularly affecting people of colour due to algorithmic bias and inaccurate matching.

Algorithmic Discrimination

Credit scoring algorithms can perpetuate racial and economic bias, denying loans to qualified applicants based on factors like postcode or shopping patterns.

Privacy Invasion

Data brokers sell personal information without consent, enabling stalking, identity theft, and unauthorised tracking of individuals' movements and behaviour.

Housing Discrimination

AI-powered tenant screening tools perpetuate historical housing discrimination, making it harder for minorities to find rental accommodation.

Key Questions for Ethical Data Use

  • Consent: Did people agree to have their data collected and used this way?
  • Transparency: Do people understand how their data is being used?
  • Fairness: Does the system treat all groups of people equally?
  • Purpose: Is the data being used to help people or exploit them?
  • Accountability: Who is responsible when things go wrong?

Remember: Behind every data point is a real person whose life can be impacted by how that data is used.

What "The Cloud" Really Is

The "cloud" is just someone else's computer! When you upload a photo to Google Photos or save a document to OneDrive, it travels across the internet to huge warehouses full of servers.

Inside a Data Centre

  • Thousands of powerful computers called servers
  • Massive air conditioning systems to keep them cool
  • Backup power generators for 24/7 operation
  • Security guards and cameras protecting your data

Key insight: Your data exists as magnetic patterns on spinning disks or electrical charges in memory chips!
